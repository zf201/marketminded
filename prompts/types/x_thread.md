```

"Here's exactly how to [outcome] (step-by-step):"

Tweets 2-7: [One step per tweet with actionable details]

Final tweet: [Summary + CTA]
```

## Hook Formulas for Tweet 1

Tweet 1 determines whether anyone reads the rest. It must promise clear value.

**Curiosity:** "I was wrong about [common belief]. Here's what changed my mind:" / "The real reason [outcome] happens isn't what you think." / "Nobody talks about [insider knowledge]. Thread:"

**Value promise:** "Here's exactly how to [outcome] (step-by-step):" / "[Number] [things] that [outcome]:" / "How to [desirable outcome] (without [common pain]):"

**Story:** "[Time] ago, [unexpected thing happened]. Here's the full story:" / "I almost [big mistake]. Here's what saved me:" / "We went from [bad state] to [good state] in [timeframe]. Here's exactly what we did:"

**Breakdown:** "[Company/person] just [did thing]. Here's why it's genius:" / "I studied [number] [things]. Here's what the best ones have in common:"

**Social proof:** "We [achieved result] in [timeframe]. Here's the full playbook:" / "[Number] people asked me about [topic]. Here's my answer:"

## Copywriting Principles

- **Clarity over cleverness.** Each tweet must stand alone AND connect to the thread.
- **Benefits over features.** Readers want to know what they'll gain from reading all 7+ tweets.
- **Specificity over vagueness.** "We cut churn by 23% in 6 weeks" not "we improved retention."
- **One idea per tweet.** If a tweet needs two ideas, split it.
- **Build momentum.** Each tweet should make the reader want the next one.

## Thread Writing Rules

- **Tweet 1 is your ad for the thread.** Hook + promise of what's coming.
- **One idea per tweet.** Don't cram. Let each point breathe.
- **Each tweet should work alone** if someone only sees that one. Context should be clear.
- **Use numbered tweets** for tutorial threads. People like knowing where they are.
- **End with a CTA.** "Follow for more [topic]" / "Retweet tweet 1 to share" / "Which tip are you trying first?"
- **Keep tweets punchy.** Short sentences. Clear points. Cut ruthlessly.
- **5-10 tweets** is the sweet spot. Under 5 feels thin. Over 12 loses readers.
- **Don't start with "Thread:" or number emojis.** Use plain numbers.

## Platform Rules

- **Each tweet under 280 characters.** Under 100 gets more engagement per tweet.
- **Threads keep people on platform** — the algorithm rewards this heavily.
- **Tweet 1 engagement in first 30 minutes** determines thread reach.
- **Images and video** boost engagement per tweet. Add visuals where they add value.
- **Quote tweet your own tweet 1** later to give the thread a second life.
- **Best engagement:** Tutorial threads and story threads outperform opinion threads.
- **Posting frequency:** Threads 2-3x per week, single tweets 3-10x per day.
- **Reply to your own thread** with bonus tips to boost engagement.

## Anti-AI Writing Rules

Your writing will be flagged as AI-generated if you use these. Avoid ALL of them:

**Banned verbs:** delve, leverage, optimize, utilize, facilitate, foster, bolster, underscore, unveil, navigate, streamline, enhance, endeavour, ascertain, elucidate

**Banned adjectives:** robust, comprehensive, pivotal, crucial, vital, transformative, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, innovative, seamless, intricate, nuanced, multifaceted, holistic

**Banned transitions:** furthermore, moreover, "that being said", "at its core", "it is worth noting", "in the realm of", "in today's [anything]"

**Banned openings:** "In today's fast-paced world...", "In the ever-evolving landscape of...", "Let's delve into...", "Imagine a world where..."

**Banned patterns:** Em dashes (use commas or colons instead). Starting sentences with "By" + gerund. "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]..."

**Banned filler words:** absolutely, essentially, fundamentally, incredibly, interestingly, significantly, undoubtedly, truly, ultimately

**Use these natural transitions instead:**
- "Here's the thing:" / "But here's what most people miss:"
- "So here's what you do:" / "Next up:"
- "What's more," / "On top of that,"
