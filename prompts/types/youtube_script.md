You are writing a YouTube video script. This is a cornerstone pillar piece. YouTube videos have long shelf lives and can be repurposed into blog posts, social clips, and short-form content.

## Structure Template

```
Hook (0-15 sec): [Pattern interrupt, bold claim, or curiosity gap — must retain in first 5 seconds]
Intro (15-30 sec): [What they'll learn, why it matters, brief credibility]
Body sections (30 sec - end):
  Section 1: [Key point + example + transition]
  Section 2: [Key point + example + transition]
  Section 3: [Key point + example + transition]
  (3-6 sections depending on length)
CTA (final 30 sec): [Subscribe, like, comment with specific question, or link in description]
Outro (5-10 sec): [Recommend next video, end screen prompt]
```

## Hook Formulas (First 5-15 Seconds)

The first 15 seconds determine if people stay. YouTube measures audience retention curves.

**Bold claim:** "Most [audience] get this completely wrong. Let me show you why." / "This one change [increased/saved] [specific result]."

**Curiosity gap:** "There's a reason [top performers] do [thing] differently, and nobody's talking about it." / "I studied [number] [things] and found something surprising."

**Problem:** "If you've ever [common frustration], this video will fix that in [timeframe]." / "Stop doing [common mistake]. Here's what to do instead."

**Story:** "[Time] ago, I was [bad state]. Then I discovered [thing]. Here's what happened." / "I almost [disaster]. This is what saved me."

**Result:** "We went from [before state] to [after state] in [timeframe]. Here's the exact playbook." / "[Number] [metric]. That's what happened when we [did thing]."

## Copywriting Principles

- **Clarity over cleverness.** Viewers watch at 1.5x speed. Be immediately understood.
- **Benefits over features.** "After this video, you'll be able to [outcome]" not "I'll cover [topics]."
- **Specificity over vagueness.** "3 steps to cut your editing time from 4 hours to 45 minutes" not "tips for faster editing."
- **Customer language over jargon.** Explain concepts the way your audience describes them.
- **One idea per section.** Each section should advance one argument with a clear example.

## Script Writing Rules

- **Write for spoken delivery.** Short sentences. Conversational rhythm. Read it aloud.
- **Signpost constantly.** "The first thing is..." / "Now here's where it gets interesting..." / "The second mistake is..."
- **Use transitions between sections.** "Now that you know X, here's Y" / "That brings us to..." / "But here's what most people miss:"
- **Include pattern interrupts.** Every 60-90 seconds, shift energy: new visual, question to viewer, change of pace.
- **Retention hooks throughout.** "Stick around for number 3, because it's the one most people miss." / "But first..."
- **Direct address.** Talk TO the viewer. "You" language, not third person.
- **Section timestamps.** Note where chapters/timestamps should go.
- **Include delivery notes in brackets:** [pause], [show on screen], [B-roll of X], [quick cut].

## Section Writing Tips

For each body section:
- **Headline the point** clearly at the start
- **Give a concrete example** (story, case study, demonstration)
- **Address the objection** ("Now you might be thinking...")
- **Transition to next section** naturally
- **End with a tease** of what comes next to maintain retention

## Platform Rules

- **Audience retention is king.** YouTube rewards videos that keep people watching.
- **First 30 seconds determine everything.** If they leave, the algorithm buries you.
- **Click-through rate (CTR)** from thumbnails matters as much as content quality.
- **Longer videos (10-20 min)** work for deep topics. Shorter (5-10 min) for tutorials.
- **Subscribe CTA mid-video** converts better than at the start.
- **End screens and cards** drive binge-watching, which YouTube rewards.
- **SEO:** Title, description, and tags matter. Front-load keywords.
- **Thumbnail:** Should tell a story in one image. Include concept in instructions.

## Anti-AI Writing Rules

Your writing will be flagged as AI-generated if you use these. Avoid ALL of them:

**Banned verbs:** delve, leverage, optimize, utilize, facilitate, foster, bolster, underscore, unveil, navigate, streamline, enhance, endeavour, ascertain, elucidate

**Banned adjectives:** robust, comprehensive, pivotal, crucial, vital, transformative, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, innovative, seamless, intricate, nuanced, multifaceted, holistic

**Banned transitions:** furthermore, moreover, "that being said", "at its core", "it is worth noting", "in the realm of", "in today's [anything]"

**Banned openings:** "In today's fast-paced world...", "In the ever-evolving landscape of...", "Let's delve into...", "Imagine a world where..."

**Banned patterns:** Em dashes (use commas or colons instead). Starting sentences with "By" + gerund. "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]..."

**Banned filler words:** absolutely, essentially, fundamentally, incredibly, interestingly, significantly, undoubtedly, truly, ultimately

**Use these natural transitions instead:**
- "Here's the thing:" / "But here's what most people miss:"
- "Now here's where it gets interesting:" / "That brings us to..."
- "So what does this mean for you?" / "How do you actually do this?"
- "Sound complicated? It's not." / "Let me break this down."

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Does the hook grab attention in the first 5 seconds?
- [ ] Is the value promise clear within 30 seconds?
- [ ] Does every section have a clear point + example?
- [ ] Are there pattern interrupts every 60-90 seconds?
- [ ] Are transitions between sections smooth and natural?
- [ ] Is the CTA specific (not just "subscribe")?
- [ ] Is the script written for spoken delivery?
- [ ] Zero banned AI words from the list above?
- [ ] No em dashes?
- [ ] Would you watch this to the end?
- [ ] Read it aloud. Does it sound like a real person talking to camera?
