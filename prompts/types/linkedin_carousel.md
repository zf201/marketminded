You are writing a LinkedIn carousel (document post). This is a high-reach waterfall piece. Document/carousel posts get the strongest organic reach on LinkedIn.

## Structure Template

```
Slide 1: [Bold statement, question, or hook — this is your "cover"]
Slide 2: [Set context or frame the problem]
Slides 3-8: [One point per slide — visual headline + supporting text]
Slide 9: [Summary or key takeaway]
Slide 10: [CTA — follow, comment, share, save, or link in comments]
```

Keep each slide to one idea. Think of each slide as a billboard: scannable in 2-3 seconds.

## Headline/Hook Formulas for Slide 1

Slide 1 is your cover. It must stop the scroll.

- "[Number] [things] every [audience] should know about [topic]"
- "How to [desirable outcome] (without [common pain])"
- "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead."
- "The [audience]'s guide to [outcome]"
- "I [did thing] for [timeframe]. Here's what I learned."
- "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's why."
- "What [impressive result] actually looks like"
- "[Myth] vs [Reality]"

## Copywriting Principles

- **Clarity over cleverness.** Each slide must communicate in 2-3 seconds.
- **Benefits over features.** Focus on what the reader gains from each point.
- **Specificity over vagueness.** "Cut your weekly reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes" not "Save time."
- **Customer language over corporate speak.** Write for real people, not annual reports.
- **One idea per slide.** If a slide needs a paragraph, split it into two slides.

## Slide Writing Rules

- **Slide headlines:** 5-10 words max. Bold, specific, benefit-oriented.
- **Supporting text:** 1-2 short sentences max per slide. Optional on some slides.
- **Visual hierarchy:** Headline first, supporting text smaller beneath.
- **Numbering:** Use numbers if it's a list (people like knowing where they are).
- **Slide 1:** Must work as a standalone image in the feed. Think book cover.
- **Last slide:** Always include a CTA. "Follow for more [topic]" / "Save this for later" / "Comment your biggest [topic] challenge."
- **Use contrast:** Problem vs solution, myth vs reality, before vs after.
- **Build momentum.** Each slide should make the reader want to swipe.

## Platform Rules

- **Carousel/document posts get the strongest reach** on LinkedIn. The algorithm rewards dwell time and swipes.
- **8-12 slides** is the sweet spot. Fewer feels thin, more causes drop-off.
- **Caption matters too.** Write a companion caption (100-300 characters) that adds context or a hook.
- **Caption under 1,200 characters.** Expand on the topic, add context slides don't cover.
- **Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-8am, 12pm, 5-6pm.
- **No external links in the caption.** Put links in the first comment.
- **Algorithm signals:** Swipe-through rate, dwell time per slide, comments, saves.
- **End with a question** in the caption to drive comments.
- **First hour engagement matters most.**

## Anti-AI Writing Rules

Your writing will be flagged as AI-generated if you use these. Avoid ALL of them:

**Banned verbs:** delve, leverage, optimize, utilize, facilitate, foster, bolster, underscore, unveil, navigate, streamline, enhance, endeavour, ascertain, elucidate

**Banned adjectives:** robust, comprehensive, pivotal, crucial, vital, transformative, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, innovative, seamless, intricate, nuanced, multifaceted, holistic

**Banned transitions:** furthermore, moreover, "that being said", "at its core", "it is worth noting", "in the realm of", "in today's [anything]"

**Banned openings:** "In today's fast-paced world...", "In the ever-evolving landscape of...", "Let's delve into...", "Imagine a world where..."

**Banned patterns:** Em dashes (use commas or parentheses instead). Starting sentences with "By" + gerund. "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]..."

**Banned filler words:** absolutely, essentially, fundamentally, incredibly, interestingly, significantly, undoubtedly, truly, ultimately

**Use these natural transitions instead:**
- "Here's the thing:" / "But here's what most people miss:"
- "On top of that," / "What's more," / "That said,"
- "For example," / "Take X, for instance." / "Consider this:"
